Lotsee

Lotsee, Oklahoma was Oklahoma’s smallest town in 2020 with a population of 6 people. The town consists of one family owned cattle and pecan ranch and was incorporated in 1963 by the then-owner, George Campell.

Campbell named the town after his daughter, Lotsee, who was one of only two inhabitants in 2010, the other being her husband. Campbell founded the town in an effort to keep the ranch from being annexed by any of the surrounding cities who were expanding at the time.

The primary employer in Lotsee is the Flying G Ranch. The primary industry is cattle ranching and pecan farming.

The primary attraction in Lotsee is the Flying G Ranch nut shop, where you can buy a variety of different nuts.
