Tulsa

Tulsa is Oklahoma’s second largest city and the 47th most populous city in the United States. Tulsa is home to several industries including oil, aviation, finance, and telecommunications. Tulsa also has a vibrant religious culture, sometimes being referred to as the buckle of the bible belt. It is also home to the only Buddhist temple found in Oklahoma.

Some of the major employers in the city include BOK Financial Corporation, American Airlines, and Consumer Affairs. However, small businesses are the primary employer in Tulsa with over 80% of people working for a business of less than 1000 people.

Tulsa is home to The Gathering Place, a massive public park open to the public featuring many different themed play areas. Attractions also include the Gilcrease Museum, an art museum with a substantial collection, and the Oklahoma Aquarium, the only free-standing aquarium in Oklahoma. Tulsa is also the birthplace of Route 66.
